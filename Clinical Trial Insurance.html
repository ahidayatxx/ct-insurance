The Opportunity for Clinical Trial Insurance in Indonesia

A Strategic Overview for Establishing a Compliant and Competitive Product

Based on analysis by dr. Ahmad Hidayat, MSc, MBA

1. The Evolving Risk Landscape of Clinical Trials

Phase I

First-in-Human

Key Risk: Unpredictable toxicity & severe adverse events in small cohorts.

Phase II

Efficacy & Safety

Key Risk: High efficacy failure rate & adverse events in patient populations.

Phase III

Large-Scale Confirmation

Key Risk: Rare/long-term AEs emerge, significant operational & multi-center risks.

Phase IV

Post-Marketing

Key Risk: Long-tail liability from rare AEs identified in real-world populations.

2. Navigating Indonesia's Regulatory Trinity

BPOM

The Mandate

Reg. 8/2024 mandates that sponsors provide compensation (can be via insurance) for trial-related injuries, making this a compliance necessity.

OJK

The Product & Financial Governance

Approves all insurance products (POJK 8/2024), governs policy wording, and enforces solvency & capital requirements for insurers.

Kemenkes

The Ethical & Healthcare Oversight

Governs Ethics Committees (KEPK), who verify insurance provisions in the Informed Consent Form and define the national standard of care.

3. The Path to an Insured Clinical Trial

1

Sponsor & Insurer

Sponsor obtains a quote and "Insurance Statement" from an insurer with an OJK-compliant product.

2

Ethics Committee (KEPK) Review

Sponsor submits protocol, ICF, and proof of insurance to the KEPK for ethical approval.

3

BPOM Application

With ethical approval, sponsor applies to BPOM for Clinical Trial Conduct Approval (PPUK).

Trial Commencement

Upon receiving PPUK, the insured trial can officially begin.

4. The Strategic Solution: Phase-Aligned Tiered Insurance

Tier 1

Phase I Focus

Essential coverage for first-in-human risk.

Tier 2

Phase II Shield

Covers emerging AEs in patient groups.

Tier 3

Phase III Comprehensive

High-limit, multi-center & global coverage.

Tier 4

Phase IV Sentinel

Addresses long-tail liability and real-world risk.

A tiered insurance product that aligns with Indonesia's risk and regulatory landscape is key to enabling safe medical innovation.
